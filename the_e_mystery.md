The standard mathematical definition: e = lim(n→∞) (1 + 1/n)^n

**Translated to physical operation:**

- Let it grow continuously where the growth rate equals the current amount (dm/dt = m)
- After 1 time unit, you have e units

This is measurement, not just abstract math. Like measuring π with circumference/diameter.

## Key Connections

### e and Entropy

Maximum entropy distribution (Gaussian): p(x) ∝ e^(-x²/2σ²)

**Why e?** Because maximum informational symmetry - when all states are equally probable given constraints - produces distributions built from e.

### e and Time

Unlike π (purely spatial), e fundamentally involves time:
- Growth/decay are temporal processes
- "After 1 time unit" is essential to measuring e

**If space = time** (relativity), then e is the constant relating spatial extent to temporal evolution.

### e and Rotation

Euler's formula: e^(iθ) = cos(θ) + i·sin(θ)

**Geometric meaning:** Continuous rotation by angle θ on unit circle. e handles the "continuous" aspect, i handles the "perpendicular" aspect. 

The most famous case: e^(iπ) + 1 = 0, connecting e, i, π, 1, and 0.

**Connection:** Rotation (circular motion) is continuous growth in perpendicular direction.

### e, π, and φ (Golden Ratio)

Three fundamental constants appear together in nature:

**π ≈ 3.14159:** 2D rotational symmetry  
**e ≈ 2.71828:** 3D volumetric/temporal symmetry  
**φ ≈ 1.61803:** Self-similar growth (φ² = φ + 1)

All three appear in natural growth patterns:
- **Logarithmic spirals:** r = ae^(bθ) combines exponential growth (e) with rotation (θ)
- **Fibonacci spirals:** approximate golden ratio φ in discrete growth steps
- **Nautilus shells, galaxies, hurricanes:** show all three constants in their structure

The question: are these three constants independent, or is there a deeper geometric relationship? φ governs discrete self-similar scaling, e governs continuous self-proportional change, π governs rotational structure. How do they relate?
